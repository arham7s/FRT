SPAN-2 Margin Engine
MODULE G — Standard Portfolio Analysis of Risk (CME SPAN-2 methodology)
What is SPAN-2? SPAN-2 is CME Group's margin methodology. It generates a grid of scenarios combining price moves and volatility shifts, then takes the worst-case loss as the scanning risk. Additional charges (spread, spot, delivery) are added on top. This replicates the actual margin calculation exchanges use.

How SPAN-2 Works
The methodology explained

Step 1 — Scanning Risk
SPAN-2 generates N×3+2 scenarios by combining price moves (from −PSR to +PSR in N steps) with volatility shifts (up, flat, down). Each cell shows the P&L of your position under that scenario. The worst-case loss across all scenarios becomes the Scanning Risk.

Step 2 — Inter-Month Spread Charge
If you hold positions across multiple contract months, a spread charge is added to capture basis risk — the risk that near and far months move differently.

Step 3 — Spot Month Charge
Positions in the spot (front) month attract an extra charge due to higher liquidity and delivery risk as expiry approaches.

Step 4 — Delivery Risk
Physically settled contracts near expiry may incur delivery risk charges reflecting the cost of actual commodity delivery.

Step 5 — Net Option Value
If you hold options, their net market value is credited (or debited) against the margin requirement. Long option premium reduces margin; short premium increases it.

Final SPAN-2 Margin = Scanning Risk + Spread Charge + Spot Charge + Delivery Risk − NOV Credit
